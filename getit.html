GO GET IT

All that talk about coffee and tea. Don't you want to go get some? You can take your beloved out for a sip or surprise them with their favorite flavor. This is were a little bit of sweet comes in. I also threw in a couple of sweet shops too.

BROOKLYNITE COFFE SHOPS

Brooklyn Roasting Company keeps getting bigger and bigger, literally. They've taken over the warehouse at 25 Jay Street in DUMBO. The space is now home to their commercial coffee roasters and grinders, a coffee counter where you can order their fair trade, organic brews, and plenty of seating space. Grab a spot on one of the big orange couches or perch yourself at one of the tables made from wood they salvaged during the renovations. This is a great place to hang out for hours.

Milk & Roses has such a lovely atmosphere When you cross the threshold, it feels like you might be entering someone's private library. The walls are full of hardbound books, a piano sits on the side of the room and inviting couches and tables and chairs are positioned throughout. There's a charming garden in the back. In the evening, Milk & Roses serves wine and delicious Italian food (the owners are Italian). They often host poetry readings and other events.

Cousin John's Cafe & Bakery is my personal favorite. Although it's not a typical coffee shop it represents where the little bit of sweet comes in. They're cakes are the best in Brooklyn. Perfect for a summer time stroll and chill. Check it out.

BROOKLYNITE TEA SHOPS

Bellocq Bellocq is a fine tea purveyor and attlier offering lovely artisan tea blends, full leaf pure teas and tea accessories. They welcome guests to visit and sample their teas.

T2 is the ultimate tea spot on Smith Street. Give it a try and you can also order online.
