WHAT'S YOUR TEA?

Whether you like your tea loose or in a tea bag there is something for you and your style of sipping. Here is a simple little guide to get you to start stipping.
Make it an experience by checking out some of the Tunes

Black Tea

Black teas are fully oxidized teas.They brew a liquor from reddish brown to dark brown. They are the most popular type of tea in the western world. Black teas range from 40-60 milligrams of caffine per 8 oz cup. Black teas from China are divided into two main categories: Northern Chinese and Southern Chinese. There are three major tea producing areas in India: Darjelling, Assam, and Nilgiri.

Oolong Tea

These teas are semi-oxidized, whcih places them midway between green and black teas. This gives them the body and complexity of a black tea, with the brightness and freshness of a green tea. The caffeine content and antioxidant level is also midway between that of green and black teas, making them most healthy and palatable. A very favortie and desired tea amongst Connoisseurs, all oonlongs hail fro meither China or Taiwan.

Green Tea

Green tea leaves plucked in the morning are ready to be brewed in a pot the same night. They bypass of oxidation allows green tea to retian most of its natural dark green color, Tannins, Vitamin C, Chlorophyll and minerals. The taste of green tea is therefore more astringent and subtler than oolong or black tea. Green tea is the perfect meditative aid: it acts as a mild stimulant, without causing insomnia or nervousness. It refreshes and quiets.

White Tea

White teas are the least processed of all teas. They release the least amount of caffeine of all teas. Almost all white teas hail from Fujian Province, China. White teas are picked when young tea buds are tightly enclosed in new leaves. This retains a silky, downy quality in the leaves. When you first drink white tea, it seems quite tastless - as if you were drinking hot water. However, after a while, you'll become aware of a subtle change in your breath and at the back of your mouth. You will taste a soft, nourishing sweetness and eventually experience a similar sensation down your throat.

Pu'erh

Pu'erh teas are aged and fermented. These aged teas are revered throughout Asia for their medicinal benefits, which range from curing hangovers to reducing cholesterol. Pu'erh tea is very smooth in taste, and can be even darker than black tea. This is a naturally permented tea, and, if stored properly, the older the tea, the better the flavor.

Yerba Mate (Pronounced "YERBA MAHTAY)

Mate is a medicinal and cultural drink of ancient origins. Introduced to the world by the Guarani Indians of South America, Mate is a species of Holly Plant, and can range in flavor from earlthy and roasted to grassy. It is a healthy and stimulating drink, an appetite suppressant and also high in Vitamin C, potassium and magnesium. The compounds in the plant have a relaxing effect on smooth muscle tissue, rather than a stimulating effect on the central nervous system.

Rooibos

Rooibos is a naturally caffeine-free herbal tea indigenous to the Cedarburg Mountain area in South Africa, also known as Red Bush Tea. It's naturally sweet flavor, lack of bitter tannins, and naturally decaf nature maket it a great tea for the whole family.
