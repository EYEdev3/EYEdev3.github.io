WHOSE YOUR COFFEE?

Here is a simple guide to the many different styles of coffee. It's for all the Coffee Lovers, Coffee Haters and the nosey & curious.
Oh yeah - don't forget to crank up some Tunes while you're drinking.

Espresso

No milk, just pure coffee. A one-once shot of intense, rich black coffee made and served immediately. Comes from the Latin word Expresere which means "to press out".

Caffe Latte a.k.a. Cafe au Lait

A Caffee Latte is a single shot of espresso with steamed milk and up to a quarter of an inchd of frothed milk on top. If in an Italian restaurant, be sure to order a Caffe Latte and not just a Latte as you may end up with just a glass of milk.

Cappuccino

Cappuccino is a drink that traditionally has equal parts espresso, steamed milk and frothed milk. Many coffee chops go with bigger is better and put more milk in.

Americano

An Americano is a single shot of espresso with 6-8 ounces of hot water added.

Hammerhead

Some call it a shot in the dark. A Hammerhead isi a shot of espresso in a coffee cup that is then filled with drip coffee.

Mocha

This is usually a cappuccino or a Cafe Late with chocolate syrup added.

Espresso con Panna

This is a shot of espresso with whipped cream.

Espresso Ristretto

This is a restricted shot of espresso. Less water is put through the coffee beans. It is the strongest and most concentrated espresso.

Expresso Lungo

There is twice as much watger allowed through the coffee.

Espresso Breve

Espresso made with light cream.

Caffe Creme

A velvety smooth coffee, brewed fresh from the bean which results in a thick mousy layer of foam called a crema.

Caffe Mocha

A layer of chocolate syrup on the bottom of the cup, topped with espresso, steamed milk and chocolate sprinkles.

Doppio

A couble shot of espresso.

Double Dry Short

A double shot of espresso in a short cup with no foam.

Espresso Macchiato

Espresso with a minimal amount of steamed milk on top.

Quad

An espresso drink with four shots of coffee.
